# Mumbai 2024: Key Insights

**Parliament vs State Assembly · Data-driven briefing**

---

## 1. Executive summary

Mumbai voters produced two different verdicts within six months. In the May 2024 Lok Sabha election, the opposition **Maha Vikas Aghadi (MVA)** won **4 of 6** parliamentary seats. In the November 2024 Vidhan Sabha election, the ruling **Mahayuti** alliance won **22 of 36** assembly seats.

**Eighteen** of thirty-six assembly areas exhibit **split-ticket** behaviour: the alliance that led in May (via the parent parliamentary seat) is not the alliance that won the assembly segment in November. Fifteen areas moved from MVA-leaning in May to Mahayuti in November; three moved the other way, mainly in the western suburbs.

Mid-day and other outlets reported that Mahayuti’s **Lok Sabha vote share in Mumbai largely held** after November, while MVA’s **assembly tally collapsed**. That pattern is consistent with many voters treating the two ballots differently, not with a single city-wide party realignment.

---

## 2. What changed between May and November?

### National frame (May)

- Voters reacted to the **2022 Shiv Sena and NCP splits**, dual symbols, and loyalty to Uddhav Thackeray vs Eknath Shinde / Ajit Pawar.
- MVA consolidated segments of **Muslim and Dalit** support in parliamentary races (e.g. Mumbai South Central, North East).
- BJP retained **Mumbai North** (Piyush Goyal) with a large margin, though reduced versus 2014/2019.
- **Mumbai North West** produced the country’s narrowest Lok Sabha margin: Shinde Sena’s Ravindra Waikar beat UBT’s Amol Kirtikar by **48 votes** after recount (Indian Express).

### Local frame (November)

- **BJP** won **15** assembly seats in Mumbai (Indian Express), versus one MP seat, driven by suburban booth networks (Borivali, Mulund, Malabar Hill, Vile Parle).
- **UBT** held **10** assembly seats despite losing the statewide battle, protecting island-city and some western pockets (Worli, Versova, Dindoshi).
- **Congress** held south Mumbai pockets (Mumbadevi, Dharavi) while losing many competitive suburbs.
- **Friendly fights** hurt MVA more than Mahayuti statewide; Mumbai had Sena-vs-Sena and multi-cornered contests (MNS fielding, Hindustan Times on vote splits).

## 3. Parliamentary constituency notes

### Mumbai South (island city)

- **May:** MVA (Sawant, UBT). **November:** Mixed: BJP (Colaba, Malabar Hill), Congress (Mumbadevi), UBT (Byculla, Shivadi, Worli).
- **Insight:** Strongest intra-PC variation; parliamentary opposition win coexists with BJP and Congress assembly wins. Supports **candidate- and ward-level** factors.

### Mumbai South Central

- **May:** MVA (Desai). **November:** Mahayuti gains in Wadala, Sion, Chembur, Anushakti Nagar; MVA holds Dharavi, Mahim.
- **Insight:** Dense working-class belt; assembly pivots toward ruling alliance despite parliamentary MVA win.

### Mumbai North Central

- **May:** MVA (Congress, Gaikwad). **November:** Mostly BJP / Shinde Sena; UBT holds Vandre East, Kalina.
- **Insight:** Airport-adjacent suburbs; BJP faces (Shelar, Alavani) outperform parliamentary Congress win.

### Mumbai North East

- **May:** MVA (Patil). **November:** BJP-heavy (Ghatkopar, Mulund); UBT Vikhroli; SP Mankhurd.
- **Insight:** Classic east-suburban flip; organisational advantage to Mahayuti.

### Mumbai North West

- **May:** Mahayuti (Waikar, 48 votes). **November:** Mostly Mahayuti but **UBT** wins Versova, Dindoshi, Jogeshwari East.
- **Insight:** Recount drama for Parliament; assembly still fragments by local face.

### Mumbai North

- **May:** Mahayuti (Goyal). **November:** Almost all BJP / Shinde; Congress Malad West exception.
- **Insight:** Highest alignment across elections; BJP suburban fortress.

## 4. Split-ticket and strategic voting

**Split-ticket** here means different alliance winners at parliamentary vs assembly level for the same area.

| Direction | Count | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| MVA (May) → Mahayuti (Nov) | 15 | Parliamentary protest / national mood; assembly reward to ruling local networks |
| Mahayuti (May) → MVA (Nov) | 3 | Personal loyalties (often UBT/Congress MLAs) outweigh parliamentary lean |

**Strategic voting** (inferred): voters using Lok Sabha to signal on national splits; using Vidhan Sabha to choose MLAs and civic delivery.

**Ideological stability** (inferred): limited city-wide flip; Mahayuti LS share stable per Mid-day while assembly seats shifted.

We do not claim proof of individual voter intent without booth-level or survey data.

## 5. Turnout

City-wide averages in the dataset: roughly **54%** (May) and **55%** (November), using district turnout proxies for some AC fields. Turnout did not collapse; **choice** changed in 18 areas more than abstention.

Affluent-tier areas in illustrative demographics sometimes show slightly larger May–November turnout variation than dense working-class tiers (exploratory chart only).

## 6. Demographics and geography (illustrative)

Charts use **estimated** community and density tiers, not census tracts.

- **Island city:** More competitive; UBT and Congress pockets persist.
- **Western suburbs:** Mahayuti majority; UBT islands where Thackeray brand remains strong.
- **Eastern suburbs:** Largest batch of MVA→Mahayuti switches in the data.

Higher estimated Muslim population share often correlates with MVA assembly performance, with exceptions (see scatter on Analysis page).

## 9. Optional outlook (speculative)

If Mahayuti maintains suburban booth density and early candidate announcements, Mumbai may continue high split-ticket rates even under varying national moods. MVA recovery likely requires fewer friendly fights and stronger assembly-ground coordination in the east and north central belts.

Civic issues (Dharavi redevelopment, flooding, metro disruption) will likely dominate assembly messaging regardless of Lok Sabha sentiment.
